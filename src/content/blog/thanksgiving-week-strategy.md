# Thanksgiving Week: Simple Strategy Guide for Models

Many of your U.S. viewers will be **traveling** or **busy with family** during Thanksgiving week.  
This means some days will have **less traffic**, and some nights will have **more**.

This guide explains everything in **simple English**, easy to translate, and easy to use.

---

# What Is Thanksgiving?

- A major U.S. holiday  
- **Date in 2025:** November 27  
- Millions of people travel or stay with family  
- Many sleep in hotels or guest rooms  
- Viewers have **less privacy during the day**, **more privacy late at night**

---

# What You Need to Know (Very Simple)

### ✔ Daytime → fewer viewers  
People are with family.

### ✔ Late night → more viewers  
People finally get privacy.

### ✔ Sunday night → very strong traffic  
People return home and relax online.

---

# Best Times to Stream

### **1. Wednesday Night (Nov 26)**  
**Best:** 11 PM–3 AM ET  
Many viewers are in hotels and bored.

### **2. Thursday Night (Nov 27)**  
**Best:** After 11 PM ET  
After family events, viewers want quiet, private time.

### **3. Sunday Night (Nov 30)** — **VERY GOOD**  
**Best:** 8 PM–1 AM ET  
Viewers are back home and comfortable.

---

# Times to Avoid

- Thursday afternoon/evening (12 PM–9 PM ET)  
- Wednesday daytime  
- Sunday morning  

If you stream during these times, keep it short (30–45 minutes).

---

# What You Should Do

### ✔ Save energy for late-night shows  
Late-night = higher privacy for your viewers.

### ✔ Announce your special schedule  
Example:  
> “This week I will be live late at night (U.S. time). Many viewers travel, so late-night shows will work best.”

### ✔ Offer a small “Thanksgiving Travel Pack”  
Short videos and photos for mobile viewing.

### ✔ Be warm and positive  
Good themes:
- Gratitude  
- Relaxation  
- Stress escape  
- Comfort and support  

---

# Easy Messages You Can Use

### Before the week
> “Many U.S. viewers travel this week. I will be live late at night when you have more privacy.”

### Thanksgiving Day
> “Happy Thanksgiving to my U.S. viewers! I will be live late tonight when you have more private time.”

### Sunday Night
> “Welcome home! I missed you. Let's enjoy a long show tonight.”

---

# Weekly Schedule Template

| Day | Best Plan |
|-----|-----------|
| **Tue** | Normal show + small late show |
| **Wed night** | Long late-night show |
| **Thu day** | Skip or short show |
| **Thu night** | Long late-night show |
| **Fri** | Normal show |
| **Sat** | Normal show |
| **Sun night** | Very strong night — long special show |

---

# Quick Checklist

**Before the week:**
- ☐ Announce late-night schedule  
- ☐ Prepare phone-friendly videos  
- ☐ DM loyal viewers  
- ☐ Test mobile lighting  

**During the week:**
- ☐ Focus on late-night U.S. times  
- ☐ Keep daytime shows short  
- ☐ Use warm, positive messages  

**After the week:**
- ☐ Thank your viewers  
- ☐ Return to normal schedule  

---

# Summary

- Thanksgiving changes viewer behavior  
- Daytime is slow  
- Late-night is best  
- Sunday night is the strongest  
- Be friendly, simple, welcoming, and clear  

This guide helps you adjust and earn more during one of the most unusual weeks of the year.
