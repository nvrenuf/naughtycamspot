# Founding Model Intake Review Workflow

This document is the internal review playbook for new Founding Model Program submissions.

Its job is simple:
- make intake review consistent
- reduce guesswork
- help Lee decide the right next step quickly
- keep the program selective without becoming vague or improvised

This is an internal operations document.
It is not public-facing copy.

## 1. Purpose

The Founding Model Program is real work in real conditions.

That means a good review should accomplish four things:
- identify what problem the applicant actually has
- map her to the correct stage of the ladder
- decide whether she is a fit now, later, or not at all
- produce a short, usable response and next-step plan

The goal is not to over-analyze.
The goal is to make a clear, defensible decision with enough structure to repeat it across the next 5 to 20 applications.

## 2. Review Stages

Use this sequence for every application.

### 1. Intake received

- confirm the lead record exists
- confirm the contact field is usable
- confirm the required fields are present

### 2. Initial qualification

- determine whether the application is serious enough to review
- check for obvious mismatch, low-effort answers, or unusable contact info

### 3. Stage mapping

- map the submission to the most likely stage:
  - Foundation / Start Right
  - Launch
  - Brand Builder
  - Growth Partner

### 4. Recommendation

- write a short internal recommendation
- include why that stage fits
- note any obvious concerns or missing info

### 5. Response decision

- accepted
- hold / need more info
- not fit right now
- possible agency/operator fit later

### 6. Response sent

- send the appropriate reply
- record the response status and next action

## 3. Inputs To Review

Review the submission using the current intake fields:

- preferred model name
- alternate model names
- closest stage
- current stage
- live status
- platform interest
- current platforms
- current handles
- main problem
- optional detail on main problem
- preferred contact method
- contact value
- notes

These are enough to make a first-pass decision if reviewed carefully.

## 4. Core Evaluation Questions

Use these questions on every review.

### Applicant state

- Is she new, relaunching, active-but-messy, generic, or growth-ready?
- Is she giving specific answers or vague answers?
- Does the submission sound serious enough to execute real changes?

### Naming and identity

- Is there a workable model name path?
- Do the alternate names suggest confusion or potential platform conflicts?
- Does the submission sound like someone who needs naming/identity cleanup first?

### Platform fit

- Is there an obvious platform fit?
- Is the stated platform interest realistic for her current stage?
- Does the combination of current platforms and live status suggest confusion or mismatch?

### Business problem

- Is the real issue traffic?
- Or is it structure?
- Or conversion?
- Or retention?
- Or lack of distinction?
- Or simple confusion about where to start?

Do not accept the applicant's wording at face value.
Translate it into the actual business problem.

### Delivery model

- Is this a strong direct-to-model fit?
- Is this a useful founding-model proof case?
- Is this more likely to be relevant later through an agency/operator model instead of as a direct buyer relationship?

### Program fit

- Is this a strong Founding Model candidate?
- Will reviewing and helping this case improve the system?
- Is the applicant likely to follow through on recommendations?

## 5. Stage-Mapping Logic

Use this as the default internal mapping guide.

### Foundation / Start Right

Map here when the application shows:
- weak naming
- weak identity
- no structure
- confusion about platform direction
- no clear front door
- no real launch plan
- "I do not know where to start" energy

Good signs for this stage:
- new or restarting
- little or no live history
- messy or unclear positioning
- likely to make preventable beginner mistakes without intervention

### Launch

Map here when the application shows:
- some clarity already exists
- but the handoff is weak once someone finds her
- public surfaces are scattered
- the front door is weak or missing
- setup exists but does not form a usable launch flow

Good signs for this stage:
- live or nearly live
- some assets or profiles already exist
- traffic might be possible, but what happens after discovery is weak
- needs front-door logic, setup cleanup, and launch structure

### Brand Builder

Map here when the application shows:
- she is active but generic
- public surfaces feel forgettable
- there is no strong memory structure
- there are no recurring event hooks
- the identity is too interchangeable with everyone else

Good signs for this stage:
- already active
- somewhat functional setup exists
- problem is differentiation, identity, recurring event logic, or discovery quality

### Growth Partner

Map here when the application shows:
- the model is already earning
- but the business feels unstable
- too much depends on random traffic
- repeat spend is weak
- retention is weak
- there is no strong recurring-event or premium-offer system

Good signs for this stage:
- established live activity
- signs of actual demand
- problem is not "how do I start?" but "how do I make this more repeatable and stable?"

## 6. Review Outputs

Every review should produce the following internal output:

- recommended stage
- short rationale
- immediate next steps
- acceptance decision
- whether naming review is needed first
- whether platform-fit review is needed first
- whether missing information must be collected before moving forward

Keep this short.
The output should be useful, not essay-length.

## 7. Decision Statuses

Use one of these internal statuses:

- Accepted – Foundation / Start Right
- Accepted – Launch
- Accepted – Brand Builder
- Accepted – Growth Partner
- Hold – Need More Info
- Not Fit Right Now
- Possible Agency/Operator Fit Later

Use the most specific status that fits.

## 8. Common Failure Modes

These are the recurring problems to watch for.

- weak naming
- no structure
- no front door
- confused platform choice
- too much given away too early
- traffic obsession without conversion logic
- no event thinking
- no retention thinking
- generic identity
- low seriousness or low execution confidence
- vague answers that hide the real problem

The review should identify the underlying failure mode, not just repeat the applicant's wording.

## 9. First Actions After Acceptance

When an applicant is accepted, start with this checklist:

1. Confirm the accepted stage.
2. Confirm the preferred contact channel.
3. Confirm the preferred model name and whether alternate names need checking.
4. Begin name / handle review if needed.
5. Begin platform-fit review if needed.
6. Identify front-door readiness:
   - what exists now
   - what is missing
   - what needs to be built or clarified first
7. Request any missing assets or context needed for the first review pass.
8. Send a concise acceptance message with the next step.

## 10. Direct-Model vs Future Operator Fit

Use this note when deciding how important a submission is commercially.

### Strong direct-to-model fit

Usually looks like:
- serious relaunch
- strong active model
- clear execution energy
- useful founding-model proof case
- obvious Brand Builder or Growth Partner potential

### Useful founding-model learning case

Usually looks like:
- early-stage but thoughtful
- likely to expose workflow gaps in naming, platform fit, or launch structure
- not yet premium direct-to-model value, but still useful for system refinement

### Possible agency/operator fit later

Usually looks like:
- the applicant herself may not be the strongest buyer
- but the pattern is commercially interesting
- suggests later operator delivery logic around onboarding, launch quality, or structured growth support

Do not force this category too early.
Use it when the signal is real.

## 12. Response Templates

These are internal templates for Lee to customize manually.

### Accepted / good fit

```text
Thanks for applying. I reviewed your intake and you look like a fit for [stage].

The main reason is that your current situation points most strongly to [short reason].

The next step is [next step].

You do not need to submit another application. I will use what you already sent and follow up here.
```

### Hold / need more info

```text
Thanks for applying. I reviewed your intake and I need one or two missing pieces before I can map you cleanly to the right stage.

Please send:
- [missing item]
- [missing item]

Once I have that, I can give you a clearer recommendation.
```

### Not fit right now

```text
Thanks for applying. I reviewed your intake and I do not think this is the right fit to move forward right now.

The main reason is [short reason].

That does not mean never. It means the current stage or setup is not strong enough yet for this program to make sense.

If your situation changes later, you can reapply.
```

### Possible agency/operator fit later

```text
Thanks for applying. I reviewed your intake and there is useful signal here, but I do not think the strongest next step is a direct move through the Founding Model Program right now.

The pattern looks more relevant to a broader operator or team workflow than a single direct-model case.

I am noting it accordingly and may circle back if that lane becomes the better fit.
```

## 13. Review Standard

A good review is:
- quick enough to repeat
- clear enough to defend
- specific enough to act on

If the output does not tell Lee what stage to map, what to say next, and what to do first, it is still too vague.
